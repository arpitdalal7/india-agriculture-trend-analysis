This project explores **agricultural production trends in India** using an interactive BI dashboard. It analyzes how different crop groups (food grains, oilseeds, pulses, and commercial crops) contribute to India’s agriculture, and how production is distributed across states and crop types.

The goal is to provide a **data-driven view of India’s agriculture** within the context of the **global and Indian economy**, and to highlight regional and crop-wise specializations.


---

## 🌍 1. Global & Indian Economic Context

### 1.1 World Economy & Agriculture

- **Global GDP (nominal):** ~USD 105 trillion  
- **Global GDP (PPP):** ~USD 180 trillion  
- **Countries covered:** 190+ (World Bank, IMF data)

**Sector-wise share of global GDP:**

| Sector      | Share of Global GDP | Examples                                  |
|------------|---------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Services   | ~65%                | IT, banking, healthcare, trade, transport |
| Industry   | ~27%                | Manufacturing, construction, mining       |

**Where agriculture fits:**

- Employs ~27–30% of the global workforce  
- Contributes ~USD 4 trillion annually  
- Backbone for:
  - Food industry  
  - Textile industry  
  - Bio-energy  
  - Rural livelihoods  

**Top agriculture-producing countries:**

China, India, United States, Brazil, Indonesia.

### 1.2 India’s Role in Global Agriculture

India is the **2nd-largest agricultural producer in the world**, after China, and plays a critical role in global food security.

Key facts:

- Ranks **2nd globally** in overall agricultural output  
- **2nd largest** producer of rice and wheat  
- **Largest** producer of milk, pulses, and spices  
- Agriculture supports **~45% of India’s population**  
- Even with a smaller overall GDP share, agriculture remains central to **India’s economy, employment, and food security**

**Sources:** FAO (FAOSTAT), World Bank

## 🌱 2. Agricultural Land Use in India

Total geographical area: **328.7 million hectares (Mha)**

Key agricultural land statistics (2021–22):

- **Total Agricultural Land:** 180.11 Mha (~54.8% of total area)  
- **Net Sown Area (NSA):** 141.0 Mha (~42.8%) – land cultivated at least once a year  
- **Gross Cropped Area (GCA):** 219.16 Mha – counts multiple cropping on the same land  
- **Cropping Intensity:** **155.4%**, showing increased multiple cropping due to improved irrigation and farming practices

## 🛡️ 4. Oilseed Production in India

The dashboard highlights production levels of major oilseed crops such as:

- Groundnut  
- Soybean  
- Rapeseed & Mustard  
- Castor seed  
- Sesame  
- Sunflower, Linseed, Safflower, Nigerseed  

### 4.1 Key Insights – Oilseeds

1. **Groundnut** has the **highest production**, making it the most important oilseed in India.  
2. **Soybean** is the **second-largest oilseed**, crucial for edible oil and protein markets.  
3. **Rapeseed & Mustard** rank third, mainly grown in **northern and central India**.  
4. **Castor seed and Sesame** show moderate production.  
5. **Sunflower, Linseed, Safflower, and Nigerseed** have very small shares, indicating limited cultivation.  
6. India’s oilseed production is **highly concentrated in a few major crops**, increasing dependence on **imports of edible oils**.

### 4.2 State-wise Groundnut Production – Key Observations

- **Gujarat** is the **largest producer** of groundnut, clearly dominating the chart.  
- **Rajasthan** ranks second but is significantly lower than Gujarat.  
- **Madhya Pradesh** stands third with moderate contribution.  
- **Tamil Nadu** has noticeable but smaller production.  
- **Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh** show relatively low production.  

> Groundnut production is concentrated in **western and central states**, with a large gap between Gujarat and others, indicating **regional specialization in oilseed cultivation**.

---

## 🌾 5. Food Grain Production

The dashboard presents **top food grains** such as:

- Rice  
- Wheat  
- Maize  
- Jowar  
- Bajra  
- Ragi  
- Barley  
- Small millets  

### 5.1 Crop-wise Observations

a. **Rice** is the **most produced food grain** and the primary staple.  
b. **Wheat** is second, especially important in **northern and central India**.  
c. **Maize** ranks third, with rising demand for **food, fodder, and industry**.  
d. **Jowar and Bajra** show moderate production, mainly in semi-arid areas.  
e. **Ragi** has lower production but high nutritional value.  
f. **Barley and small millets** have minimal output and limited cultivated area.  

> Food grain production in India is **heavily concentrated in rice and wheat**, which together form the majority share.

### 5.2 State-wise Rice Production – Key Insights

- **Uttar Pradesh** is the **largest producer** of rice, supported by large cultivated area and irrigation.  
- **Punjab** ranks second with very high productivity due to modern practices.  
- **West Bengal** is third, benefiting from favorable climate and rainfall.  
- **Madhya Pradesh and Telangana** show almost equal production, reflecting expansion.  
- **Chhattisgarh and Odisha** are key rice-growing states in eastern India.  
- **Bihar, Haryana, and Andhra Pradesh** contribute relatively smaller shares.  

> Rice production is **regionally concentrated**, driven by **water availability and climate**.

---

## 🥗 6. Pulses Production Distribution

Crops included: **Gram, Tur (Arhar), Urad, Moong, Lentil, Other pulses**

### Key Insights – Pulses

- **Gram** is the **largest contributor**, ~40% of total pulse output.  
- **Other pulses** together form the second-largest share (~22%), indicating diversity.  
- **Tur (Arhar)** contributes about **16%**, the third most important pulse.  
- **Urad (Black gram)** accounts for ~8–9%.  
- **Moong (Green gram)** contributes nearly 8%.  
- **Lentil** has the **smallest share** (<5%).  

> Pulse production is dominated by **Gram and Tur**, though a variety of other pulses are cultivated across regions.

---

## 🏭 7. Commercial Crop Production

Commercial crops analyzed: **Sugarcane, Cotton, Jute & Mesta, Guarseed, Tobacco, Sannhemp**

### 7.1 Crop-wise Observations

a. **Sugarcane** dominates commercial crop production, contributing **~88%** of total output.  
b. **Cotton** is second but far behind sugarcane.  
c. **Jute & Mesta** together account for **~5–6%**.  
d. Individually, Jute and Mesta have smaller shares.  
e. **Guarseed, Tobacco, and Sannhemp** show negligible production.  

> Commercial crop production in India is **highly concentrated in sugarcane**, making the sector sensitive to **water availability and price policies**.

### 7.2 State-wise Commercial Crop Production – Insights

- **Uttar Pradesh** is the **largest producer**, with ~50% of total among selected states.  
- **Maharashtra** ranks second (~30%), mainly due to sugarcane.  
- **Karnataka** contributes around 12–13%.  
- **Gujarat** has a smaller but significant share.  
- **Bihar and Tamil Nadu** contribute the least.  

> Commercial crops are concentrated in a few states, influenced by **climate, irrigation, and sugar industry presence**.

## 🗺️ 8. Spatial Distribution of Agricultural Production (Map Insights)

The dashboard map visualizes **state-wise production intensity** across India.

### Key Spatial Insights

a. There is **wide spatial variation**, indicating strong regional specialization.  
b. **Northern states** (Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana) show **high agricultural activity** due to fertile alluvial soils and irrigation.  
c. **Central India** (Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh) contributes significantly to **food grains and pulses**.  
d. **Western states** (Maharashtra, Gujarat) are important for **cash crops and oilseeds**.  
e. **Southern states** (Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka) show strong output driven by irrigation projects and diversified cropping.  
f. **Eastern India** (West Bengal, Odisha, Bihar, Assam) is prominent in **rice and jute**, supported by high rainfall.  
g. **North-Eastern states** have relatively lower production due to **hilly terrain and limited cultivable land**.  
h. **Coastal and island regions** (Goa, Andaman & Nicobar Islands) show low intensity because of geographical constraints.  
i. Marker sizes reveal that production is **concentrated in a few key states**, not evenly distributed.  

> Overall, climate, soil, irrigation, and land availability are the major drivers of spatial patterns in Indian agriculture.

---

## 🧠 9. Project Learnings

- India’s agriculture is **diverse but concentrated** in a few crops (rice, wheat, sugarcane, groundnut, gram).  
- Production is **regionally specialized**, with each zone playing a distinct role (e.g., north for wheat & sugarcane, east for rice & jute, west for oilseeds).  
- Heavy reliance on certain crops (e.g., **sugarcane, groundnut, rice**) introduces **risk** related to water scarcity and price fluctuations.  
- Pulses and millets, though low in volume, are **nutritionally important** and key for future diversification and sustainability.
